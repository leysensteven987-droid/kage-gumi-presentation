The problem

The knowledge is scattered

Everything you need to keep one bike right exists — it's just spread across a 700-page PDF nobody opens, a forum thread from 2014, a receipt in a drawer, and memory. When the question is "what torque, and when did I last do this?", none of those answer in under ten minutes.

Tetsu is a single place for one specific motorcycle: what it is, what's been done to it, what's due, and what the factory actually says.

What it does

Garage, book, log, manual

01

The garage

The bike itself — specs, fluid capacities and grades, torque figures — in one reference card instead of scattered across sources of varying trust.

02

Service intervals

What's due and when, measured against real mileage rather than a vague sense that it's probably been a while.

03

Maintenance logbook

Every job recorded with date, mileage and notes. The log is what makes the intervals meaningful, and what makes a future sale honest.

04

Build book

Every modification, with its part and its price. Paste an accessory link and it fills in the picture, name and price by itself — quick-add, because friction is what stops a log being kept.

05

The workshop manual, openable

The full factory service manual as searchable text — case-insensitive full-text search across every chapter, not a PDF you scroll.

06

Ask Tetsu

A chat grounded in this bike's data plus the relevant manual passages — so a torque figure comes out of the book rather than out of a language model's memory.

Grounding is the whole point of the chat. A general assistant will confidently give you a torque spec for the wrong model year. Tetsu retrieves the passage from the actual manual and answers from it — the difference between a plausible number and a correct one is a stripped thread.

Design

Monochrome metal

Tetsu was rebranded away from the crew's dark-and-crimson chrome into an identity of its own. The first attempt reached for ember orange, a stylised skull and hazard stripes — the obvious motorcycle clichés — and was rejected outright.

What replaced it: black, gunmetal, brushed steel and bone, with a chrome gradient as the only thing that could be called an accent. Status colours are deliberately desaturated so they read as function, not decoration. The 鉄 character is the hero mark and the watermark. No colour. No skull.

Honest state

What's built

Shipped and in use: the garage, service schedule, logbook, build book with link preview, the searchable workshop manual, photo slots, and the grounded chat. It is an installable app on a phone, which is where it gets used — standing next to the bike, not at a desk.

  • It documents exactly one motorcycle. Multi-bike support isn't built, and isn't currently wanted.
  • The chat depends on a local assistant being installed; without it the feature degrades to a note rather than an error.
  • The manual corpus is one model year. Other years would need their own ingestion.
